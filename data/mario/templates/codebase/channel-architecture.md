```markdown
# Channel Architecture

**Audit Date:** 2025-06-15

## Active Channels

**Owned Channels:**
- Company blog: SEO-driven educational content, 2-3 posts/week
- Email list (12,000 subscribers): Weekly newsletter + automated sequences
- Website: Product pages, landing pages, documentation
- YouTube: Tutorial videos and product walkthroughs, 1-2/month

**Earned Channels:**
- Guest posts: 1-2/month on industry publications
- Podcast appearances: Quarterly outreach to relevant shows
- Customer reviews: G2, Capterra, Trustpilot (organic)

**Paid Channels:**
- Google Ads: Search campaigns targeting high-intent keywords, $3k/month
- LinkedIn Ads: Sponsored content for B2B lead generation, $2k/month
- Retargeting: Display ads via Google Display Network, $500/month

**Shared/Social Channels:**
- LinkedIn (company + founder): Thought leadership, 3-5 posts/week
- Twitter/X: Industry commentary and product updates, daily
- Instagram: Behind-the-scenes and culture, 2-3 posts/week

## Content Distribution Flow

**Creation:**
- Marketing team writes blog posts and email sequences
- Founder creates LinkedIn thought leadership content
- Freelance writer handles 2 SEO articles/month
- Designer creates visual assets in Figma

**Review & Approval:**
- Blog: Writer -> Editor -> SEO review -> Publish
- Email: Writer -> Marketing lead approval -> Schedule
- Social: Creator -> Brand voice check (async) -> Post

**Distribution:**
- Blog post published -> Email newsletter features summary -> Social posts promote with key quotes -> Top performers get paid promotion budget
- Lead magnets promoted via blog CTAs, social posts, and dedicated landing pages

**Repurposing:**
- Long-form blog -> 3-5 LinkedIn posts + Twitter thread + email summary
- Webinar -> YouTube recording + blog recap + social clips + email follow-up
- Customer interview -> Case study page + testimonial quotes + social proof assets

## Funnel Stages

**Awareness:**
- Channels: Blog/SEO, LinkedIn organic, Google Ads (brand + top-of-funnel), guest posts
- Goal: 50,000 monthly website visitors, 10% growth quarter-over-quarter
- Key content: How-to guides, industry trend pieces, comparison articles

**Consideration:**
- Channels: Email nurture (5-email welcome sequence), case study pages, retargeting ads
- Goal: 3% visitor-to-lead conversion, 500 new leads/month
- Key content: Lead magnets (templates, checklists), webinars, detailed product guides

**Decision:**
- Channels: Product demo page, pricing page, sales email sequence, free trial
- Goal: 10% lead-to-customer conversion
- Key content: Product tours, ROI calculator, customer testimonials, comparison pages

**Retention:**
- Channels: Onboarding email sequence (7 emails over 14 days), help center, monthly product newsletter
- Goal: <5% monthly churn, NPS >50
- Key content: Getting started guides, feature announcements, success tips, community highlights

## Channel Interdependencies

**Primary Flows:**
- SEO blog -> Email signup (sidebar + exit intent popup) -> Welcome sequence -> Product trial CTA
- LinkedIn thought leadership -> Profile link -> Website -> Retargeting pixel -> Display ads
- Google Ads (search) -> Dedicated landing page -> Lead magnet download -> Email nurture -> Sales page

**Support Relationships:**
- Email newsletter drives 30% of returning blog traffic
- Customer testimonials from retention program feed into ads and landing pages
- Social engagement metrics inform blog topic selection
- Blog content repurposed as email and social content (single source of truth)

## Attribution Model

**Model Type:** First-touch attribution via UTM parameters, supplemented by self-reported "How did you hear about us?" on signup

**Tracking Setup:**
- UTM parameters on all external links (campaigns, social, email, ads)
- Google Analytics 4 with conversion events for signup, trial start, purchase
- CRM (HubSpot) captures UTM source on first form submission, carries through pipeline
- Google Ads conversion tracking pixel on thank-you pages

**Known Gaps:**
- No multi-touch attribution (considering HubSpot multi-touch reports)
- Podcast and word-of-mouth attribution relies on self-report only
- Cross-device journeys not tracked (user starts on mobile, converts on desktop)
- Dark social sharing (DMs, Slack) not captured
```

<guidelines>
**What belongs in CHANNEL-ARCHITECTURE.md:**
- All active marketing channels organized by type (owned, earned, paid, shared)
- Content creation and distribution workflows
- How channels map to funnel stages (awareness, consideration, decision, retention)
- Channel interdependencies and how they feed each other
- Attribution model and tracking setup
- Content repurposing patterns

**What does NOT belong here:**
- Specific content pieces or campaign details (those are operational)
- Detailed analytics data (that's PERFORMANCE-METRICS.md)
- Tool configurations (that's TOOLS-AND-PLATFORMS.md)
- Brand voice specifics (that's BRAND-GUIDELINES.md)
- Content inventory (that's CONTENT-INVENTORY.md)

**When filling this template:**
- Review website for all active channels (check footer links, social icons)
- Check email platform for active sequences and newsletter setup
- Review social media profiles for posting frequency and content types
- Check ad platform accounts for active campaigns
- Map the typical customer journey from first touch to purchase
- Identify how content flows between channels (repurposing patterns)
- Document the attribution model and any tracking gaps

**Useful for marketing planning when:**
- Identifying which channels to prioritize for a campaign
- Understanding where content gaps exist in the funnel
- Planning content repurposing strategies
- Evaluating channel ROI and interdependencies
- Onboarding new marketing team members
- Deciding where to allocate budget increases
</guidelines>
