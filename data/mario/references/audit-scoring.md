# Website Audit Scoring System

Single source of truth for all audit agents. Every score in an audit traces back to this document.

## 6 Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Agent Assignment |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Content & Messaging | 25% | mario-website-auditor (content) |
| Conversion Optimization | 20% | mario-website-auditor (conversion) |
| SEO & Discoverability | 20% | mario-website-auditor (seo) |
| Competitive Positioning | 15% | mario-website-auditor (competitive) |
| Brand & Trust | 10% | mario-website-auditor (trust) |
| Growth & Strategy | 10% | Derived by synthesizer from cross-cutting patterns |

## Rating Scale

| Score | Rating | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 0-20 | Critical | Fundamental issues requiring immediate attention |
| 21-40 | Significant Gaps | Major weaknesses undermining effectiveness |
| 41-60 | Functional | Works but underperforming versus potential |
| 61-80 | Solid | Performing well with room for improvement |
| 81-100 | Best-in-Class | Industry-leading execution |

## Dimension 1: Content & Messaging (25%)

Evaluates how effectively the website communicates value to visitors.

### Subcriteria

| Subcriteria | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| Value Proposition Clarity | 25% | Is the core value immediately obvious? Can a visitor understand what you do in 5 seconds? |
| Message-Audience Fit | 25% | Does messaging speak to specific pain points? Does it use the audience's language? |
| Content Quality | 20% | Is copy well-written, scannable, and free of jargon? Are headlines compelling? |
| Content Completeness | 15% | Are all key pages present (about, pricing, features, contact)? Are there content gaps? |
| Call-to-Action Effectiveness | 15% | Are CTAs clear, compelling, and strategically placed? Do they match visitor intent? |

### Scoring Guide

- **0-20:** No clear value proposition. Generic or confusing messaging. Missing critical pages.
- **21-40:** Value proposition exists but buried or unclear. Messaging is generic, doesn't address specific audiences.
- **41-60:** Value proposition visible but not compelling. Content covers basics but lacks depth or personality.
- **61-80:** Clear value proposition above the fold. Messaging speaks to audience pain points. Good content coverage.
- **81-100:** Instantly clear, differentiated value proposition. Messaging is persona-specific and compelling. Rich, strategic content.

## Dimension 2: Conversion Optimization (20%)

Evaluates how effectively the website converts visitors into leads or customers.

### Subcriteria

| Subcriteria | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| Conversion Path Clarity | 25% | Is the desired action obvious? Are there clear next steps at every point? |
| Form & CTA Design | 20% | Are forms optimized (minimal fields, clear labels)? Are CTAs visually prominent? |
| Social Proof & Trust Signals | 20% | Are testimonials, case studies, logos, or reviews strategically placed? |
| Friction Reduction | 20% | Are objections addressed proactively? Is pricing transparent? Is the process clear? |
| Mobile Conversion Experience | 15% | Do conversion paths work on mobile? Are forms mobile-friendly? |

### Scoring Guide

- **0-20:** No clear conversion path. No forms or CTAs. No social proof.
- **21-40:** Basic contact form exists. CTAs are generic ("Submit"). No social proof near decision points.
- **41-60:** Clear primary CTA. Some social proof. Forms functional but not optimized.
- **61-80:** Multiple conversion paths for different intents. Strong social proof. Optimized forms.
- **81-100:** Sophisticated conversion optimization. Persona-specific CTAs. Strategic social proof placement. A/B tested elements.

## Dimension 3: SEO & Discoverability (20%)

Evaluates how well the website is optimized for search engines and organic discovery.

### Subcriteria

| Subcriteria | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| Technical SEO | 25% | Page speed, mobile-friendliness, structured data, sitemap, robots.txt |
| On-Page SEO | 25% | Title tags, meta descriptions, heading hierarchy, keyword usage, internal linking |
| Content Strategy for SEO | 20% | Blog/resource presence, topic coverage, content freshness, keyword targeting |
| Authority Signals | 15% | Backlink indicators, domain authority signals, brand mentions, PR coverage |
| Local/Industry SEO | 15% | Industry-specific optimization, local listings (if applicable), niche directories |

### Scoring Guide

- **0-20:** Major technical issues. No meta tags. No content strategy. Site may not be indexed properly.
- **21-40:** Basic technical SEO. Some meta tags but not optimized. No content strategy.
- **41-60:** Technically sound. Decent on-page SEO. Some content but no clear strategy.
- **61-80:** Strong technical foundation. Well-optimized pages. Active content strategy with keyword targeting.
- **81-100:** Excellent technical SEO. Comprehensive on-page optimization. Strategic content program driving organic growth.

## Dimension 4: Competitive Positioning (15%)

Evaluates how effectively the website differentiates from competitors.

### Subcriteria

| Subcriteria | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| Differentiation Clarity | 30% | Is it clear why this product/service vs alternatives? What's the unique angle? |
| Competitive Awareness | 25% | Does messaging address competitive context without naming competitors? |
| Market Positioning | 25% | Is the market position clear (premium, value, niche, etc.)? Is pricing consistent? |
| Unique Value Communication | 20% | Are unique features/benefits prominently featured? Are they believable? |

### Scoring Guide

- **0-20:** No differentiation. Could be any company in the space. Generic positioning.
- **21-40:** Some differentiation claimed but not supported. Positioning unclear.
- **41-60:** Differentiation exists but not compelling. Competitive advantages mentioned but not prominent.
- **61-80:** Clear differentiation with proof points. Strong positioning with consistent messaging.
- **81-100:** Unmistakable market position. Differentiation is the first thing visitors understand. Strong moat narrative.

## Dimension 5: Brand & Trust (10%)

Evaluates how effectively the website builds trust and communicates brand identity.

### Subcriteria

| Subcriteria | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| Visual Brand Consistency | 25% | Consistent design language, typography, color palette, imagery style |
| Trust Indicators | 25% | Security badges, certifications, partner logos, media mentions, team photos |
| Brand Personality | 20% | Does the site have a distinctive personality? Does it feel human? |
| Transparency | 15% | Is the company transparent about team, pricing, process, and policies? |
| Professional Polish | 15% | Quality of design, absence of errors, attention to detail |

### Scoring Guide

- **0-20:** Inconsistent or missing branding. No trust signals. Looks unprofessional.
- **21-40:** Basic branding exists but inconsistent. Few trust signals. Generic feel.
- **41-60:** Consistent basic branding. Some trust signals. Professional but unremarkable.
- **61-80:** Strong brand identity. Multiple trust indicators. Distinctive personality.
- **81-100:** Exceptional brand experience. Comprehensive trust ecosystem. Memorable and distinctive.

## Dimension 6: Growth & Strategy (10%)

*Derived by synthesizer from cross-cutting patterns across all 5 audited dimensions.*

### Subcriteria

| Subcriteria | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| Growth Readiness | 25% | Is the site ready to scale? Are foundations in place for growth marketing? |
| Strategic Alignment | 25% | Do all elements work together toward a coherent strategy? |
| Quick Wins Available | 25% | How many high-impact, low-effort improvements exist? |
| Long-term Potential | 25% | What's the ceiling? How much improvement is possible? |

### Scoring Guide

- **0-20:** No coherent strategy. Site works against growth. Major foundational work needed.
- **21-40:** Some strategic elements but disconnected. Growth would require significant rework.
- **41-60:** Basic strategy in place. Growth possible with moderate effort. Some quick wins available.
- **61-80:** Clear growth strategy. Well-positioned for scaling. Several optimization opportunities.
- **81-100:** Growth-optimized. Strategic coherence across all dimensions. Mainly fine-tuning needed.

## Composite Score Calculation

```
Composite = (Content * 0.25) + (Conversion * 0.20) + (SEO * 0.20) +
            (Competitive * 0.15) + (Trust * 0.10) + (Growth * 0.10)
```

## Recommendation Priority

Recommendations should be prioritized by:

1. **Impact:** How much will this improve the composite score?
2. **Effort:** How difficult is this to implement?
3. **Dependencies:** Does this unlock other improvements?

Priority matrix:
- **P1 (Do Now):** High impact, low effort
- **P2 (Plan):** High impact, high effort
- **P3 (Quick Win):** Low impact, low effort
- **P4 (Backlog):** Low impact, high effort
